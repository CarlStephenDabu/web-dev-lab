Eat more Plant Based Meals

Increased plant consumption benefits society by fostering food security and sustainability, the environment by lowering carbon emissions and preserving resources, and health by supplying vital nutrients and preventing disease.

Here, why is important to eat more plants.

    For your Health

  • Nutrients and Fiber: Vitamins, minerals, and fiber found in fruits, vegetables, legumes, and whole grains help with digestion, reduce cholesterol, and maintain a healthy body.
  • Disease Prevention: Diets high in plants lower the risk of diabetes, heart disease, obesity, and some types of cancer.
  • Weight & Energy: Plant-based diets are typically lower in calories and bad fats, which can help you manage your weight and provide you with consistent energy.

    • For the Environment

  • Reduced Carbon Footprint: Compared to rearing animals, growing plants requires less energy, water, and land.
  • Biodiversity: Eating more plant-based foods helps protect ecosystems and lessens deforestation.
  • Sustainability: It promotes a food system that uses fewer resources to feed more people.

    • For Society

  • Food Security: Increasing the production of plant-based foods can aid in the global fight against hunger.
  • Ethical Considerations: Selecting plants lessens dependency on factory farming and the moral dilemmas associated with the suffering of animals.
